ACV-3A2 "MARSHAL"

UNITED HOLLOW REPUBLIC — Amphibious Combat Vehicle

Overview

The ACV-3A2 "MARSHAL" is the United Hollow Republic’s primary expeditionary amphibious combat vehicle, developed to support rapid-response operations across coastal, riverine, and fractured urban environments. Building on lessons learned from earlier variants, the A2 introduces a redesigned hull and turret, improved survivability systems, and a fully integrated battlefield network suite. Notably, the 3A2 now operates with a crew-less turret, keeping the crew better protected within the hull and allowing for advanced electronics.

The platform emphasizes operational flexibility. Its hybrid diesel-electric powertrain allows for extended range, reduced thermal signature during low-speed movement, and silent approach capabilities in contested zones. An adaptive suspension and traction system enables high-speed maneuvering on land while maintaining stability during amphibious transitions and open-water navigation.

Protection systems combine layered composite armor with modular reactive elements and an active protection suite capable of intercepting incoming projectiles. Internally, the vehicle is designed to maximize crew survivability, with blast-mitigating seating, automated fire suppression, and compartmentalized damage control.

The "MARSHAL" is also equipped for network-centric warfare. Integrated sensor arrays, drone support systems, and real-time data sharing allow it to function as both a frontline combat vehicle and a mobile command node. This enables coordinated infantry deployment, reconnaissance, and precision engagement in both conventional and asymmetric combat scenarios.
